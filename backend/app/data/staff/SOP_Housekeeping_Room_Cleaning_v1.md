# SOP – Housekeeping: Room Cleaning & Turnover
Southern Horizons Hotel – Hobart, Tasmania  
Version: 1.0 | Last updated: Nov 2025

## 1. Purpose
To provide a consistent, hygienic and safe standard for cleaning guest rooms.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all room attendants and housekeeping supervisors.

---

## 3. Room Cleaning Types

- **Departure Clean** – Guest has checked out.
- **Stayover Service** – Guest remains in-house.
- **Deep Clean** – Scheduled periodically or after special cases.

---

## 4. Departure Clean – Step-by-Step

1. **Preparation**
   - Knock three times and announce:  
     > “Housekeeping.”  
   - If no response, enter and announce again.
   - Check for personal belongings left behind.

2. **Strip the bed**
   - Remove all linen and pillowcases.
   - Place used linen in the linen bag.

3. **Waste removal**
   - Empty all bins and recycle where possible.
   - Remove any remaining room service items.

4. **Bathroom cleaning**
   - Apply cleaner to toilet, basin, shower and tiles.
   - Clean from **cleanest to dirtiest** (e.g. mirror → basin → shower → toilet).
   - Replace amenities:
     - Soap, shampoo, conditioner, body wash.
     - Tissues, toilet paper, sanitary bags.

5. **Bedroom area**
   - Dust all surfaces (desk, bedside tables, lamps).
   - Wipe high-touch areas:
     - Remote controls
     - Light switches
     - Door handles
   - Check wardrobe, drawers, and safe.

6. **Bed making**
   - Use fresh linen and pillowcases.
   - Ensure bed is properly aligned and neat.

7. **Floor**
   - Vacuum carpet or mop hard floor.
   - Check under the bed and furniture.

8. **Final check**
   - Ensure curtains/blinds are neat and open halfway.
   - Set room temperature to standard (e.g. 21–22°C).
   - Switch off lights and close the door securely.
   - Mark room as “Clean/Inspected” in the system.

---

## 5. Stayover Service – Summary

1. Knock and announce “Housekeeping.”
2. Confirm if guest is happy for service at that time.
3. Make bed (unless “Do Not Disturb” sign is present or guest declines).
4. Refresh towels if they are left on the floor.
5. Top up amenities (water, tea/coffee, bathroom items).
6. Remove rubbish.
7. Quick vacuum or sweep as required.

---

## 6. Lost Property

1. Label all found items with:
   - Date
   - Room number
   - Description
2. Log items in the Lost Property register.
3. Store in the designated locked cupboard.
4. Follow the hotel’s retention policy (e.g. 3 months).

---

## 7. Safety & WHS (Housekeeping Specific)

- Avoid lifting heavy items alone – ask for assistance.
- Use wet floor signs when mopping.
- Never leave chemicals unattended around children or guests.
- Report any hazards (e.g. damaged furniture, exposed wires) immediately to Maintenance and Supervisor.
