# SOP – Guest Complaints & Service Recovery
Southern Horizons Hotel – Hobart, Tasmania  
Version: 1.0 | Last updated: Nov 2025

## 1. Purpose
To ensure guest complaints are handled professionally and consistently, turning problems into opportunities for loyalty.

---

## 2. Principles

- Listen first.
- Empathise and apologise.
- Take ownership (even if the issue was not directly your fault).
- Act quickly.
- Follow up.

---

## 3. Handling a Complaint – L.E.A.R.N. Model

**L – Listen**  
Allow the guest to explain the problem fully.

**E – Empathise**  
Acknowledge their feelings:  
> “I’m very sorry this has happened. I understand how frustrating that must be.”

**A – Apologise**  
Say sorry on behalf of the hotel, without blaming other departments.

**R – Resolve**  
Offer a practical solution within your authority:
- Room move if there is a major issue (noise, cleanliness, fault).
- Late check-out or amenity where appropriate.
- Involve duty manager for serious cases.

**N – Notify**  
Log the incident in the system and notify the relevant department.

---

## 4. Typical Complaint Scenarios

### Noise Complaint
1. Apologise for the disturbance.
2. Check if there are noisy neighbours or external works.
3. Offer:
   - Room move if available.
   - Ear plugs if a move is not possible.
4. Log the incident and inform Security / Duty Manager if required.

### Cleanliness Issue
1. Apologise and thank the guest for bringing it to our attention.
2. Arrange immediate re-clean or room move.
3. Follow up by phone once action is completed.
4. Inform Housekeeping Supervisor for review and training.

### Service Delay (e.g. late room service)
1. Apologise for the delay.
2. Provide an updated time estimate.
3. Consider a gesture of goodwill (e.g. complimentary drink or small discount) if delay is significant.
4. Review processes with the F&B team afterwards.

---

## 5. When to Escalate

Escalate immediately to the Duty Manager when:

- Guest is extremely upset or aggressive.
- Safety or security is involved.
- There is a potential legal or media risk.
- Complaint relates to discrimination or harassment.

---

## 6. Documentation

- Log all complaints in the hotel’s incident/complaints system.
- Include:
  - Date and time
  - Guest details and room number
  - Summary of complaint
  - Actions taken
  - Follow-up required
