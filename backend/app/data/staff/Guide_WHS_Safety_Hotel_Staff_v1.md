# Guide – Work Health & Safety (WHS) for Hotel Staff
Southern Horizons Hotel – Hobart, Tasmania  
Version: 1.0 | Last updated: Nov 2025

## 1. Purpose
To outline key WHS responsibilities and procedures for staff in all departments.

---

## 2. General WHS Responsibilities

- Take reasonable care of your own health and safety.
- Take reasonable care not to adversely affect the health and safety of others.
- Follow all training, signage and instructions.
- Report hazards, incidents and near-misses promptly.

---

## 3. Common Hazards in Hotels

- Slippery floors (lobby, bathrooms, kitchens).
- Manual handling (luggage, linen, stock).
- Chemical use (cleaning products).
- Electrical equipment.
- Aggressive or intoxicated guests.

---

## 4. Manual Handling

- Bend your knees and keep your back straight when lifting.
- Avoid twisting while carrying loads.
- Use trolleys or luggage carts where possible.
- Ask for help if an item is too heavy or awkward.

---

## 5. Slips, Trips & Falls

- Clean spills immediately.
- Use wet floor signs.
- Keep corridors clear of obstacles.
- Report damaged flooring or loose carpets.

---

## 6. Chemical Safety

- Always read labels and Safety Data Sheets (SDS).
- Wear appropriate PPE (gloves, goggles) as required.
- Never mix chemicals.
- Store chemicals in approved areas only.

---

## 7. Emergency Procedures

### Fire
1. Remain calm.
2. Follow the RACE principle:
   - **R** – Remove people from immediate danger.
   - **A** – Alert others / raise alarm.
   - **C** – Confine fire/smoke if safe (close doors).
   - **E** – Extinguish if trained and safe, or evacuate.
3. Follow evacuation routes to assembly point.
4. Assist guests, especially those with mobility issues.

### Medical Emergency
1. Call emergency services as per hotel policy (e.g. 000 in Australia).
2. Notify Duty Manager.
3. Do not move the person unless they are in immediate danger.
4. Provide first aid only if you are trained and it is safe.

---

## 8. Incident Reporting

- Report all incidents, injuries and near-misses to your supervisor as soon as possible.
- Complete the Incident Report Form before the end of your shift.
