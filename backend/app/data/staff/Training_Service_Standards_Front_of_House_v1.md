# Training – Service Standards for Front of House
Southern Horizons Hotel – Hobart, Tasmania  
Version: 1.0 | Last updated: Nov 2025

## 1. Our Service Promise

We aim to provide warm, genuine and efficient service that reflects Tasmanian hospitality.

---

## 2. Appearance & Grooming

- Uniform clean, pressed and complete.
- Name badge visible at all times.
- Hair neat; long hair tied back.
- Minimal jewellery and fragrance.
- Phone use restricted to staff areas.

---

## 3. Communication Standards

- Use polite language: “please”, “thank you”, “my pleasure”.
- Avoid slang in front of guests.
- Never argue with a guest.
- If you do not know the answer:
  > “That’s a great question, let me check that for you.”

---

## 4. Phone Etiquette

- Answer within 3 rings where possible.
- Greeting example:
  > “Good evening, Southern Horizons Hotel, this is [Your Name], how may I assist you?”
- Smile while speaking – it can be heard in your voice.
- Ask permission before placing a caller on hold.

---

## 5. Handling Special Requests

- Record all requests clearly in the PMS.
- Repeat the key details to the guest to confirm.
- Give a realistic timeframe.
- Follow up or hand over to the next shift if not completed.

---

## 6. Local Knowledge

Front of House staff should know basic information about:

- Key Hobart attractions:
  - Salamanca Market (Saturdays).
  - MONA (Museum of Old and New Art) – ferry from Brooke Street Pier.
  - Mount Wellington / kunanyi lookouts and walking tracks.
  - Battery Point historic area.
- Approximate travel times to:
  - Hobart Airport.
  - City centre / waterfront.
- Nearby essentials:
  - Pharmacies.
  - Supermarkets.
  - ATMs.

If unsure, use maps or official visitor information to assist the guest.
