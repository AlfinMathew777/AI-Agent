# Local Area – Attractions, Food & Things To Do
Southern Horizons Hotel – Hobart, Tasmania  
Version: 1.1 | Last updated: Nov 2025

## Salamanca & Waterfront

- **Salamanca Market** (Saturdays) – Local food, crafts and music.
- Waterfront area – Restaurants, bars and harbour views.
- Good for:
  - Casual dining.
  - Evening drinks.
  - Short walks along the docks.

---

## MONA – Museum of Old and New Art

- One of Tasmania’s most famous attractions.
- Ferry departs from **Brooke Street Pier** on the waterfront.
- Allow at least half a day.
- Tickets and ferry times are available online or at the pier.

---

## Mount Wellington / kunanyi

- Panoramic views over Hobart and the River Derwent.
- Walking tracks, lookouts and photo spots.
- Weather can change quickly – bring warm clothing even in summer.

---

## Battery Point

- Historic area with cottages, cafes and short walks.
- A few minutes’ walk uphill from Salamanca.

---

## Food & Restaurant Areas Near the Hotel

If you want to try food outside the hotel, these areas are popular:

### Salamanca & Waterfront
- Wide range of restaurants and bars.
- Good for seafood, modern Australian and casual dining.

### North Hobart (Elizabeth Street)
- “Food strip” with many small restaurants and cafes.
- Mix of Italian, Asian, modern Australian and takeaway options.

### Hobart CBD Laneways
- Small cafes and lunch spots open mainly on weekdays.

Reception can suggest options based on:
- Budget (cheap eats vs special occasion).
- Dietary needs (vegetarian/vegan/gluten-free).
- How far you want to walk or if you prefer a short taxi ride.

---

## Day Trips – Ideas

- Bruny Island – Local produce, scenery and wildlife tours.
- Richmond – Historic bridge, buildings and cafes.
- Tasman Peninsula – Dramatic coastline and walking tracks.

For directions, weather and current opening hours, please ask Reception.
