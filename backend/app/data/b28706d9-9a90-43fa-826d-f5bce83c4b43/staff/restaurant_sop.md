# Restaurant & Food Service - Standard Operating Procedures

## Service Standards

### Guest Greeting
1. Acknowledge guest within 30 seconds of arrival
2. Smile and make eye contact
3. Welcome warmly: "Good [morning/afternoon/evening], welcome to [Restaurant Name]"
4. Ask for reservation name or party size
5. Seat promptly or provide accurate wait time

### Table Service Sequence
1. **Seating**: Pull out chairs, present menus
2. **Water service**: Within 2 minutes of seating
3. **Beverage order**: Within 3 minutes
4. **Appetizer order**: Within 5 minutes
5. **Entree order**: After appetizer service
6. **Check on guests**: 2 minutes after food delivery
7. **Clear plates**: When all guests are finished
8. **Dessert/coffee offer**: After clearing
9. **Present check**: When requested or after dessert
10. **Thank and farewell**: As guest departs

### Timing Standards
- Beverages: Served within 3-5 minutes
- Appetizers: Served within 10-12 minutes
- Entrees: Served within 15-20 minutes
- Desserts: Served within 8-10 minutes

## Dietary Restrictions & Allergies

### Common Allergens
- Peanuts and tree nuts
- Dairy products
- Gluten/wheat
- Shellfish and fish
- Eggs
- Soy
- Sesame

### Allergen Protocol
1. **Always ask**: "Does anyone have food allergies?"
2. **Take seriously**: Never assume or dismiss
3. **Communicate**: Inform kitchen immediately
4. **Verify**: Double-check with chef before serving
5. **Document**: Note in order system
6. **Separate preparation**: Use clean utensils and surfaces

### Medical Emergency Response
If guest shows allergic reaction:
1. Ask if they have an EpiPen
2. Call 911 immediately
3. Alert manager and security
4. Keep guest calm and monitor
5. Document incident

## Menu Knowledge

### Standard Menu Items
Staff must know for each dish:
- All ingredients
- Preparation method
- Portion size
- Allergens present
- Available modifications
- Recommended wine pairings

### Wine Pairing Basics
- **White meat/fish**: White wines (Chardonnay, Sauvignon Blanc)
- **Red meat**: Red wines (Cabernet, Merlot)
- **Spicy dishes**: Off-dry wines (Riesling, Gewürztraminer)
- **Desserts**: Sweet wines (Port, Moscato)
- When in doubt, recommend sommelier consultation

### Daily Specials
- Review specials at pre-shift meeting
- Know preparation, ingredients, and price
- Suggest specials to guests actively

## Upselling Techniques

### Professional Suggestions
- "May I suggest our [item] to complement your [order]?"
- "Our [special] is excellent today, prepared by our chef with..."
- "Would you like to add [item] to enhance your dish?"
- Describe items with sensory words (fresh, crispy, creamy)

### Appropriate Opportunities
- Appetizers: When presenting menu
- Side dishes: With entree order
- Premium options: Suggest upgraded items
- Desserts: After clearing entrees
- After-dinner drinks: With dessert or check

### What NOT to Do
- Never be pushy or aggressive
- Don't upsell if guest seems budget-conscious
- Avoid suggesting items guest has declined
- Never make guest feel pressured

## Complaint Handling

### Immediate Response
1. Listen without interrupting
2. Apologize sincerely
3. Take ownership: "I will fix this for you"
4. Offer solution immediately
5. Act quickly
6. Follow up within 5 minutes

### Common Issues & Solutions
- **Food temperature**: Remake immediately
- **Wrong order**: Replace immediately, comp if delayed
- **Long wait**: Apologize, explain, comp appetizer/drink
- **Poor service**: Manager involvement, comp consideration
- **Foreign object in food**: Manager must handle, document, comp meal

### When to Get Manager
- Guest requests to speak with manager
- Complaint involves safety or health
- Guest is dissatisfied with offered solution
- Situation is escalating
- Potential legal or liability issue

## Food Safety

### Temperature Control
- Cold food: Below 40°F (4°C)
- Hot food: Above 140°F (60°C)
- Danger zone: 40-140°F - limit time to 2 hours

### Hygiene Standards
- Wash hands every 30 minutes minimum
- Wash hands after: bathroom, handling cash, touching face/hair
- Use gloves when handling ready-to-eat food
- Never work when ill (fever, vomiting, diarrhea)

### Food Handling
- First In, First Out (FIFO) for storage
- Label all containers with date
- Never serve dropped food
- Taste with clean spoon (never double-dip)
- Keep raw and cooked items separate

## Room Service

### Order Taking
1. Answer within 3 rings
2. Greet warmly with department and name
3. Verify room number and guest name
4. Repeat order back to guest
5. Provide estimated delivery time
6. Thank guest

### Delivery Standards
- Deliver within promised time (typically 30-45 minutes)
- Hot food must be hot, cold must be cold
- Check order completeness before leaving kitchen
- Knock, announce "Room Service"
- Set up tray/table properly
- Explain dishes and condiments
- Inform of tray pickup procedure

### Tray Pickup
- Guest can call for pickup or place in hallway
- Collect trays within 2 hours of placement in hallway
- Check hallways every hour after 8 PM
